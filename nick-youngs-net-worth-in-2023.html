Nick Young, a former NBA player famous for his flashy style, scoring talent, and captivating personality, gained notoriety for his basketball skills and relationship with rapper Iggy Azalea.

As of 2023, his net worth is $25 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth, earned primarily from his successful basketball career, which generated nearly $39 million in earnings.

In this article, we will delve into his net worth,  salary, NBA career, personal life and more.

Nick Young’s Earnings in the NBA

Nick Young’s income during his 12-year NBA tenure varied depending on his team and contract negotiations.

His journey began with the Washington Wizards, who selected him as the 16th overall pick in the 2007 NBA draft.

At that time, he inked a four-year deal worth $7.4 million with the Wizards. During his time there, Young demonstrated his scoring abilities, averaging 10.9 points per game.

In 2012, he transitioned to the Los Angeles Clippers, playing a pivotal role in their return to the playoffs after a six-year absence.

This move came alongside a one-year contract valued at $5.6 million, where he continued to make his mark with an average of 10.6 points per game.

2013 marked his return to his hometown team, the Los Angeles Lakers, signing a one-year deal worth $1.2 million.

The following year, he re-committed to the Lakers on a more substantial four-year contract amounting to $21.5 million.

However, Young’s most substantial contract came in 2018 when he signed a one-year agreement worth $5.2 million with the Golden State Warriors, ultimately clinching his first and only NBA championship in 2018.

In 2019, Young played his final NBA game with the Denver Nuggets on a non-guaranteed contract.

Nick Young’s Early Life and Education

Born on June 1, 1985, in Los Angeles, California, to parents Charles and Mae Young, Nick Young faced early adversity when his older brother, Charles Young Jr., fell victim to gang violence at just five years old.

This tragic loss deeply affected Young, leading to a period of admission to a mental institution.

Nick Young’s journey in basketball commenced at an early age, eventually leading him to Cleveland High School in Reseda, California, where his impressive performances earned him the distinction of being the seventh-best player in the country, as recognized by HoopScoop.

In 2003, Young earned initial team honors and secured a spot among the top seniors, according to Athlon Sports Communications.

He further solidified his status as one of the top recruits of 2004, according to prep basketball expert Frank Burlison.

Nick Young’s Current Age and College Career

Nick Young, as of August 2023, stands at the age of 38, having been born on June 1, 1985, in Los Angeles, California. During his college years, he played for the USC Trojans from 2004 to 2007.

His remarkable skills earned him a spot on the All-Pac-10 First Team for both the 2005–06 and 2006–07 seasons.

In the 2007 NCAA Tournament, Young played a pivotal role in guiding the fifth-seeded Trojans to the Sweet Sixteen, despite ultimately falling to the first-seeded North Carolina (74–64).

Notably, Young led the Trojans in scoring with 22 points during an unforgettable game against the Texas Longhorns, securing an 87–68 victory.

This performance came against none other than National Player of the Year, Kevin Durant.

Subsequently, Young opted to forego the remainder of his college career and entered the NBA draft, where he was selected by the Washington Wizards as the 16th overall pick in the first round.

Nick Young’s NBA Career Highlights

Over his illustrious 12-year NBA career, Nick Young showcased his flashy style and scoring prowess, earning a dedicated fan following.

He famously bestowed upon himself the moniker “Swaggy P” after creating his Twitter handle under that alias.

Additionally, Young took center stage in the documentary ‘Second Chance Season,’ which delves into his challenges, accomplishments, and moments of glory.

Some of the key highlights and accolades from his career include:

  • NBA champion in 2018.
  • Two-time First-team All-Pac-10 selection in 2006 and 2007.
  • Career statistics of 11.4 points, 2.0 rebounds, and 1.0 assists per game.

Nick Young’s Personal Life and Relationships

Nick Young’s personal life has seen several significant relationships.

He dated Puerto Rican track and field athlete Carol Rodriguez from 2006 to 2012, culminating in an engagement in August 2012, which regrettably ended just three months later.

Subsequently, Young embarked on a romantic journey with Australian rapper Iggy Azalea from 2013 to 2016.

Their engagement, which was celebrated on Young’s 30th birthday in 2015, concluded in 2016 following the leak of a video in which Young admitted to infidelity, courtesy of his teammate D’Angelo Russell.

Young’s love life continued with a relationship with R&B vocalist and occasional rapper Paloma Ford, lasting from August 2016 to January 2017.

Presently, Young is engaged to Keonna Green, his high school sweetheart, with whom he shares three children – two sons born in 2012 and 2019 and a daughter born in 2016.

Interestingly, Young is related to the renowned rapper Kendrick Lamar.
